Organisation Administration

Org Admin Guide

How to set up and run GoWarmCRM for your team — your people and structure, your sales process, the diagnostics that drive the action queue, your AI and integrations, and the modules your org uses.

No sections match your search. Try a different term.

01 Opening Org Admin

Org Admin is a dedicated workspace, separate from your everyday CRM. You enter it by switching your active role — your normal Member view stays exactly as you left it.

Org Admin console
The Org Admin console. A grouped left nav drives a settings panel on the right.
  1. Switch your role to Org Admin from the user card at the bottom of the main sidebar, then open Org Admin (🔑). Only members with the Owner or Admin role see this option.
  2. The grouped left nav organises every setting into sections — Team, Sales Process, Sales Execution Insights, Auto Action Execution, Data Quality, General, and Modules.
  3. The content panel shows the screen you've selected, with a title and one-line description at the top so you always know where you are.
🧭

This guide follows the nav. Each section below maps to a screen in that left nav, in the same order. Use the search box up top to jump straight to a setting by name.

💡

A sensible setup order. If you're configuring a new org from scratch: start with Members and Stages, then tune Deal Health and Diagnostic Rules (these power the action queue), connect your CRM, and finish by enabling the modules your team needs.

02 Members & roles

The Members screen is your team roster. Here you set each person's role, assign them to a department, and see how active they are.

Org Admin · Members
  1. The role legend explains what each of the four system roles can do — a quick reference while you assign them.
  2. Change anyone's role from the inline dropdown on their row. Admins can manage members but can't change an Owner; the Owner can change anyone.
  3. Assign a department (Sales, Legal, Implementation, Customer Support, Finance, Executive). Departments route work — for example, handovers and cases flow to the right people.

The four roles

RoleCan do
👑 OwnerFull control — org settings, billing, all data. Cannot be removed.
🔑 AdminManage members, invitations, integrations, and all CRM data.
👤 MemberFull CRM access — deals, contacts, emails, AI. Cannot manage users.
👁 ViewerRead-only access to all CRM data. Cannot create or edit records.
ℹ️

The Members screen also shows summary stats for your team at the top, and each row shows the join date and a running actions count so you can see engagement at a glance.

03 Hierarchy

Hierarchy is your reporting structure — who reports to whom. It's what gives managers visibility into (and edit access over) their team's deals and prospecting work.

Set each person's manager to build the reporting tree. Once it's in place, a manager can see and act on their direct and indirect reports' items, rolling up the whole branch beneath them. This structure also feeds team reporting and the manager-visibility controls used elsewhere in the product.

💡

Hierarchy vs. Teams. Hierarchy is the vertical chain of command (manager → report). Teams are horizontal groupings by segment, product, geography, or motion. Most orgs use both.

04 Teams & dimensions

Teams let you group users along the dimensions that matter to your business — market segment, role, product line, geography, and sales motion. Team Dimensions is where you define the vocabulary those groupings use.

Team Dimensions

Before you build teams, set up the dimensions you want to organise by. Each dimension has a label (for example, Market Segment or Region) and its set of values. GoWarmCRM keeps separate dimension vocabularies for your internal teams and for customer-side teams, so you can model both sides of a deal accurately.

Teams

With dimensions defined, create teams and assign members to them. Teams give you a way to slice reporting, scope work, and segment your motion without forcing everyone into a single rigid hierarchy.

ℹ️

Set up Team Dimensions first — teams reference the dimension values you create there, so defining the vocabulary up front keeps your team structure consistent.

05 Invitations

Invite new people to your organisation by email. Each invite carries the role they'll get when they join.

  1. Under New Invitation, enter the colleague's email and (optionally) a short personal message.
  2. Choose the role they'll receive on joining, then send. They get a link to register and auto-join your org.
  3. Track sent invites in the list below — pending invitations stay visible until they're accepted or expire.
💡

Inviting is the self-serve path — the person sets their own password. If you'd rather create an account directly with a temporary password, an account can also be provisioned for you by a platform super admin.

06 Playbooks

Playbooks are the reusable plays your team runs — the structured steps and templates behind deals, prospecting, CLM, and more. The Playbooks screen is where you manage which playbooks exist and how they're organised.

Use Playbook Types to organise plays by the area they belong to. Playbooks then supply the templates the rest of the product draws on — for example, the step templates that the action queue uses, and the STRAP plays that strategic planning generates from.

ℹ️

Playbooks connect to several other screens: the Actions AI settings decide whether AI enhances playbook-generated steps, and the per-module configuration can seed starter plays for you (see Configuring a module).

07 Stages

Stages define your pipelines. GoWarmCRM keeps two separate stage sets — one for deals and one for prospecting — so each pipeline reflects how that work actually flows.

  1. Pick the pipeline you're editing — Deal stages or Prospect stages.
  2. Add a stage by name (for example, Technical Review for deals or Cold Outreach for prospecting), then drag to reorder so the sequence matches your process.
⚠️

Stages ripple outward. Several diagnostics and your CRM stage-mapping depend on these stages. If you rename or reorder stages after going live, review your Salesforce/HubSpot stage mapping so synced records still land in the right place.

08 Organization roles

These are the functional roles people play on deals, prospects, and plays — distinct from the four system roles that control product permissions. Think buyer-side and seller-side roles like Economic Buyer, Champion, or Solutions Engineer.

The screen separates System Roles (the built-in set GoWarmCRM ships with) from Custom Roles (ones you add). Add a custom role by name, and deactivate any system role you don't use so it stops cluttering pickers across deals, prospecting, and playbooks.

ℹ️

Organization roles appear wherever you assign people to a deal or account — they're how multi-threading and stakeholder coverage get measured.

09 Product catalog

The Product Catalog holds the products and services your team can add as line items on deals. A well-kept catalog keeps deal values consistent and makes reporting trustworthy.

Organise products into Product Groups (with their own parent/level labels, e.g. Product Line), and define each product's billing frequency, fee type, list price, and unit label. Search and status filters help you keep a large catalog tidy, and inactive products can be retired without deleting their history.

10 Deal Health

Deal Health scores every open deal so reps and managers can see which deals are solid and which need attention. You control how that score is calculated — across six dimensions, each made of signals you can weight and toggle.

Org Admin · Deal Health
Six dimensions, each a set of toggleable signals.
  1. On the Parameters tab, expand each of the six dimensions to see its signals.
  2. Toggle which signals contribute. A dimension's switch turns the whole group on or off.
  3. Switch to the Weights tab to set how much each dimension counts toward the overall score — so what matters most to your business carries the most weight.

The six dimensions

DimensionWhat it measures
Close Date CredibilityWhether the close date is real — buyer-confirmed, hasn't slipped, tied to a buyer event.
Buyer Engagement & PowerEconomic buyer identified, exec meeting held, multi-threaded across stakeholders.
Process CompletionLegal/procurement engaged, security/IT review started.
Deal Size RealismValue vs. segment average, recent expansion, buyer-approved scope.
Competitive & Pricing RiskCompetitive deal, price sensitivity flagged, discount approval pending.
Momentum & ActivityNo buyer meeting recently, response times slower than the historical norm.
💡

The AI Usage tab shows token consumption for any AI used in health scoring, so you can keep an eye on cost.

11 ICP Scoring

ICP Scoring defines your Ideal Customer Profile and scores prospects against it. A good ICP model tells reps where to spend their time — and feeds the prospecting diagnostics that flag low-fit outreach.

Org Admin · ICP Scoring
  1. Group your criteria into categories (for example, Firmographics, Technographics, Intent) and set a weight for each in the Weight Distribution.
  2. Give each category a baseline score — the starting point before any rules adjust it.
  3. Add scoring rules. Each rule looks at a field, applies a match condition, and adds or subtracts points for match, no match, and empty cases.
ℹ️

Each prospect is scored across all enabled categories. Within a category the score starts at the baseline, then each rule nudges it up or down — so you can express both "must-haves" and "nice-to-haves" precisely.

12 Diagnostic Rules

Diagnostic Rules are the thresholds that drive GoWarmCRM's action queue. Every night (and in real time where noted), the platform checks your data against these numbers and raises actions when something needs attention. Tuning them tunes how much your team gets nudged.

Org Admin · Diagnostic Rules
Sample thresholds shown. Each module has its own group.
  1. The Edit tab holds the thresholds; the Summary tab gives a read-only overview of everything that's configured.
  2. Each module — Deals, Cases, Handovers, Prospecting, Accounts, STRAP — has its own group of numeric thresholds. Adjust a value to make a check stricter or looser.
  3. Save to apply. Changes take effect on the next nightly run (and immediately for the real-time checks).

What the thresholds cover

ModuleExample thresholds
💼 DealsStagnant days (real-time & nightly), close-imminent window, high-value threshold.
🎧 CasesStale case days, pending-customer days.
🤝 HandoversNo-kickoff days, stalled days.
🔭 ProspectingStale outreach days, ghosting days, hot-lead response window, low-ICP threshold, wrong-channel attempts/response.
🏢 AccountsStale account days, expansion-stalled days, renewal window, whitespace minimums (roles & contacts).
🎯 STRAPMinimum age before a strategic plan is re-validated.
⚠️

These shape your team's daily workload. Tighter thresholds surface more actions sooner; looser ones reduce noise. If reps say the queue is too noisy (or too quiet), this is the screen to adjust.

13 AI Agent

The AI Agent framework lets GoWarmCRM go beyond suggestions and propose actions your team can review and approve. This screen controls what the agent is allowed to do and shows how its proposals are performing.

Enable the agentic capabilities you want, and review the proposal stats — counts of proposals that are Pending, Executed, Rejected, Failed, or Expired, plus a running Total. The screen also surfaces token usage so you can track the cost of agentic work.

💡

Watch the Rejected and Failed counts early on — a high rejection rate usually means a setting needs tightening or your playbooks need refining before you let the agent do more.

14 AI Providers

Choose which AI provider and model power your org's AI features, manage your API keys, and decide whether users can bring their own keys.

  1. Pick a provider and model — Anthropic (Claude), OpenAI (GPT-4o mini), Grok (xAI), or a custom gateway endpoint.
  2. Add your organisation's API key. When an org key is set, your org uses it; otherwise GoWarmCRM falls back to the platform default.
  3. Set the user policy for whether individuals may configure their own provider keys.
ℹ️

The screen clearly shows whether you're on Org key set or Using platform fallback, and warns you about unsaved changes before you navigate away.

15 Actions AI

Actions AI decides where AI enhances the actions GoWarmCRM generates from your rules and playbooks. You turn enhancement on per action type, and choose how strategic plans (STRAPs) get written.

Org Admin · Actions AI
  1. Toggle AI enhancement per action type. Deal and STRAP actions benefit most; Contract and Prospecting actions are off by default because those plays are already well structured.
  2. Pick a STRAP generation mode: Both (rep compares AI and playbook side-by-side and chooses), Playbook only (fast, consistent, no AI call), or AI only (AI writes it, falling back to the playbook if AI is unavailable).
ℹ️

Enhancement here relies on the provider you set in AI Providers. If no AI is available, GoWarmCRM safely falls back to the playbook template.

16 AI Usage

A running dashboard of your org's AI consumption — useful for budgeting and for spotting which features are driving cost.

You'll see total tokens, estimated cost, and average tokens per call over a selectable window, a breakdown by call type, and a daily history. Use the time-range control to compare recent days against your baseline.

💡

If cost climbs, cross-reference this screen with Actions AI and AI Agent — those are the biggest levers on token spend.

17 Duplicates

Duplicate detection keeps your contacts and accounts clean by flagging likely duplicates based on rules you control.

Configure which match signals count — for example, email match or domain match — and set the visibility scope (such as own contacts only vs. across the org). When records match, GoWarmCRM surfaces them so they can be merged rather than silently piling up.

ℹ️

Cleaner data makes every other feature better — health scores, ICP scoring, and diagnostics all assume your records aren't fragmented across duplicates.

18 Workflows

Workflows are your org's data-integrity rules and automations for deals, contacts, and accounts. This is the org-scoped counterpart to the platform workflows a super admin manages.

⚙️
Workflows & Rules — a visual canvas for building rules with sequential gates, branching, and dependencies.
📋
Execution Log — a history of workflow runs and any open violations, so you can verify automations and catch records that fell out of policy.
ℹ️

These run within your organisation only. They complement — rather than override — the platform-level workflows configured by super admins.

19 Email Settings

Control which emails GoWarmCRM syncs and how they get matched to deals, prospects, and accounts. Good filters keep noise (newsletters, no-reply addresses, internal chatter) out of your CRM timeline.

Add filter entries — for example, addresses or patterns to exclude — and define your internal domains so the system can tell internal from external correspondence. Your platform super admin may have set baseline filters that apply to every org; you can add your own on top of those, but you can't remove the platform defaults.

💡

Start permissive, then tighten. It's easier to add an exclusion when you spot noise than to discover that an over-aggressive filter hid an important thread.

20 Tracking Domain

A per-customer tracking domain keeps email open/click tracking aligned with your sending reputation, instead of a shared one. It's a single DNS record on a domain you own.

  1. Choose the subdomain you want to use for tracked links.
  2. Add the CNAME record GoWarmCRM shows you to your domain's DNS.
  3. Come back and verify — once the record resolves, tracked links route through your own domain.
⚠️

DNS changes can take time to propagate. If verification doesn't pass immediately, wait and retry rather than re-creating the record. Until it's verified, tracking still works on the default domain.

21 Custom Fields

Custom fields let you capture data unique to your business on the records you care about. Definitions are scoped — they can apply to an entity type or to a specific campaign — so you only see the fields that are relevant where you are.

Create field definitions, import options in bulk by CSV, and the fields become available in the relevant record views and column pickers. Custom field values also feed the personalization context, so the data you capture here can flow into outreach.

ℹ️

Because custom fields can power both reporting and personalization, name them clearly and consistently — future-you (and your reps) will thank you.

22 Integrations

Connect the tools your team already lives in — email, calendar, cloud files, and meeting transcripts — so GoWarmCRM can sync the right context automatically.

Email & Calendar

Enable org-wide connections for Microsoft (Outlook email, calendar, OneDrive files) and Google (Gmail, Google Calendar, Google Drive). Turning these on lets your members link their accounts for email sync and calendar events. You can also scope visibility (for example, own accounts only).

Meeting & Transcript Integrations

Configure transcript providers — such as Zoom, Teams, and Fireflies — so meeting recordings and transcripts flow into the relevant deals and contacts, ready for analysis.

23 Salesforce & HubSpot

GoWarmCRM sits on top of your existing CRM. Connect Salesforce or HubSpot to keep records in sync, and map your stages and fields so data lands where you expect.

☁️ Salesforce

Connect your Salesforce org to sync contacts, accounts, deals, and leads. Records sync nightly at 04:00 UTC. Configure stage mapping and field mapping per org so Salesforce values translate cleanly to your GoWarmCRM pipeline.

🟠 HubSpot

Connect your HubSpot portal to sync companies, contacts, and deals, also on the nightly 04:00 UTC schedule, with the same per-org stage and field mapping.

⚠️

Write-back is gated. Syncing reads from your CRM by default. Writing changes back to Salesforce must first be allowed at the platform level by a super admin — then you enable it here. If you don't see a write-back option, ask your platform admin to enable the capability.

ℹ️

Review your CRM stage mapping whenever you change Stages — the two need to stay aligned for synced records to land correctly.

24 Org Settings

The organisation's basics — its name, your plan and limits, and ownership.

  • Organisation Name — your org's display name across the product.
  • Plan & Limits — your current plan, status, user seats, and member-since date (read-only here; plan and seats are set by your platform admin).
  • Prospecting Email Limits — sending limits that keep your outreach within healthy bounds.
  • Transfer ownership — hand the Owner role to another member when leadership changes.
⚠️

Ownership transfer is significant. The Owner has full control and can't be removed by others. Transfer it deliberately, and only to someone who should hold top-level control of the organisation.

25 Enabling modules

Modules are the major capability areas — Prospecting, CLM, Handover, Service, and Agency. The Modules screen is where you turn on the ones your team uses.

Org Admin · Modules
  1. Enable a module to add it to your team's product — its nav entry and features appear for members.
  2. A module the platform hasn't provisioned for your org appears locked. If you need it, ask your platform super admin to provision it (see the Super Admin guide's module section).
ℹ️

Enabling a module here adds its own configuration screen to the nav — covered next.

26 Configuring a module

Once enabled, each module gets its own settings screen. They share a common shape — a General area and a Seed panel — plus settings specific to that module.

⚙️
General — turn the module on/off and set module-wide options. Prospecting's General also includes policies like campaign deletion by owners and whether managers can edit team members' items.
🌱
Seed — load starter data (sample plays, stages, or configuration) so a freshly enabled module isn't empty. A fast way to get a module usable on day one.

Module-specific settings

ModuleExtra settings
🎯 ProspectingSkill inputs, sending schedule, prospecting playbook, Call Settings & Twilio calling setup, escalation policy, and enrichment.
📄 CLME-signature configuration and contract templates.
🤝 HandoverHandover workflow options.
🎧 ServiceGeneral configuration and SLA tiers.
🏢 AgencyClient portal and team configuration.
💡

Setting up calling? Prospecting's Twilio panel provisions a calling subaccount and a browser softphone for your org. Pair it with Call Settings to choose how calls connect (bridge, softphone, or both).
