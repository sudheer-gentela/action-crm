Super Admin Guide

Everything you need to run GoWarmCRM at the platform level — provisioning organisations, managing seats and modules, supporting customers, and controlling settings that cascade to every org.

01 Accessing Platform Admin

Platform Admin is a separate workspace from your everyday CRM. You reach it by switching your active role — your normal Member view is never replaced, just set aside while you administer the platform.

app.gowarmcrm.com
The role switcher. Your role badge lives in the user card at the bottom of the sidebar.
  1. Click your user card at the bottom-left of the sidebar.
  2. In the popover, choose Super User. Your available roles depend on your account — only people listed as platform super admins will see this option.
  3. The sidebar switches to the Platform Admin workspace and opens Platform Admin (⚡). To return to your CRM, switch back to Member the same way.
ℹ️

Who gets Super User? Super admin access is held in a dedicated platform list, completely separate from any single organisation. It is granted and revoked under Super admins — not through an org's member roles.

📋

Everything you do here is recorded. Org changes, user changes, support-mode sessions, and setting changes are all written to an immutable Audit Log. This is by design — it protects both customers and your team.

03 Managing organisations

The Organisations tab is where most of your day-to-day work happens. It lists every organisation on the platform with search, filters, and quick actions on each row.

Platform Admin · Organisations
Sample organisations shown.
  1. Search filters the list by organisation name as you type.
  2. Status and Plan dropdowns narrow the list — combine them to find, say, all trial orgs on the Starter plan.
  3. + New Organisation opens the create dialog (see Creating an organisation).
  4. Click an organisation's name to open its detail drawer.
  5. The row actions give you one-click controls — covered just below.

Row actions

👁
View details — opens the organisation's detail drawer (same as clicking the name).
🔧
Support mode — enter the org as a support session to see what they see. Always logged. See Support mode.
⏸
Suspend — freezes the organisation. Members can't use the product until you reactivate. Suspended rows are visually muted.
▶
Reactivate — appears in place of Suspend for an already-suspended org, restoring access.
🗑
Delete — permanently removes the organisation. This is destructive and irreversible — see the warning below.
⚠️

Deleting an organisation is permanent. To prevent accidents, the confirmation dialog asks you to type the organisation's exact name before the Delete button activates. Prefer Suspend whenever you only need to pause access — it's fully reversible.

04 Creating an organisation

Use + New Organisation on the Organisations tab to provision a brand-new customer workspace. You set the basics here; everything else — members, modules, integrations — is configured afterwards from the detail drawer.

Fields you'll set

FieldWhat it does
NameThe organisation's display name across the platform.
PlanOne of Free Starter Pro Enterprise. Sets the commercial tier; you can change it later.
Max UsersThe seat cap. The members list shows usage as active / max.
NotesInternal notes visible only to super admins (e.g. account context, special terms).
💡

New orgs start clean. After creating one, open its detail drawer to add the first user (or send an invite), then provision the modules the customer has bought. A module the customer hasn't been provisioned simply won't appear for their team.

05 Inside an organisation

Clicking an organisation opens its detail drawer — the control panel for that single customer. It has three working areas: organisation details, members, and module provisioning.

Organisation detail
The detail drawer slides in from the right over the organisations list.

Organisation details

Click ✏️ Edit 1 to change the Name, Plan, Max Users (seat cap), and internal Notes. Save writes the change immediately. The read-only view also surfaces the org ID, status, seat usage, and creation date.

Members & invites

The members area 2 gives you two ways to add people:

➕
Create User — set first/last name, email, a temporary password (use the 🎲 button to generate one), and a role. The person is prompted to change their password on first login.
📧
Invite — send (or copy) an invite link for an email + role. Links expire in 7 days; the recipient registers through the link and auto-joins the org.

For existing members you can change their role from the inline dropdown or remove them with the ✕. Org-level roles are Owner Admin Member Viewer.

ℹ️

Org roles vs. platform roles. These roles control what a person can do inside their own organisation. They are unrelated to platform Super Admin access, which is managed separately under Super admins.

06 Provisioning modules

Modules are the major capability areas of GoWarmCRM. As a super admin you control which modules an organisation is allowed to use; the org's own admin then decides which allowed modules to actually turn on.

In the detail drawer's Modules section 3, flip the Allowed toggle for each module. There are two distinct states worth understanding:

🔓
Allowed (you control) — whether the platform has provisioned this module for the org. This usually maps to what the customer has purchased.
✅
Enabled (org admin controls) — whether the org has switched the module on for their team. You see this as a read-only indicator.
🔁

Disallowing forces off. If you turn Allowed off for a module the org had enabled, the platform automatically forces its Enabled state off too. Re-allowing it later lets the org turn it back on — it doesn't re-enable automatically.

The five modules

ModuleWhat it adds
🎯 ProspectingProspect pipeline, outreach sequences, ICP scoring, and sender accounts.
📄 Contract Lifecycle ManagementCreate contracts, legal review, e-signature, and versioning.
🤝 Sales → Implementation HandoverA structured handover workflow on deal close — checklist, stakeholders, commitments.
🎧 Customer Support & ServiceCase management, SLA tiers, playbook-driven workflows, and team assignment.
🏢 Agency Client ManagementManage client accounts — portals, team scoping, and outreach tracking.

07 Support mode

Support mode lets you enter an organisation to troubleshoot from the inside — seeing the product the way that customer's admin sees it. It's the fastest way to diagnose a "it doesn't work for us" report.

  1. On the Organisations tab, click the 🔧 Support mode action on the org's row.
  2. Confirm the prompt. You'll see a confirmation that support mode is active for that organisation.
  3. Work through the issue, then exit when you're done.
📋

Support mode is always logged. The confirmation prompt reminds you of this before you start. Every session is written to the Audit Log with the target organisation — so use it for genuine support and diagnostics.

08 Super admins

The Super Admins tab manages who else holds platform-level access. Grants and revocations here are what actually turn the Super User role on or off for a person.

Platform Admin · Super Admins
  1. To grant access, enter the person's email, add an optional note for context, and choose Grant access. They gain the Super User role on their next session.
  2. To revoke, use the Revoke button on their row. Revoked entries are kept in the list (marked revoked) so the history stays visible.
🛡️

You can't revoke yourself. Your own row shows the action disabled — a safeguard against locking the platform out. Ask another super admin if you need your own access changed.

09 The audit log

The Audit Log is the platform's immutable record of every super-admin action. It's your accountability trail — useful for support reviews, security checks, and answering "who changed this and when?".

Each entry records the action taken, the target (org or user), who performed it, and when. Typical events include creating or editing organisations, suspending or deleting orgs, creating or removing users, granting or revoking super-admin access, and entering support mode.

📋

It's append-only. Entries can't be edited or deleted from the interface — that's what makes the log trustworthy. Treat it as the source of truth when reconstructing what happened on an account.

10 Platform workflows

The Workflows tab is where you build automation that operates at the platform level — rules that apply across organisations rather than inside any single one. It has two sub-views.

⚙️
Workflows & Rules — a visual canvas for defining platform-scope rules and their branching logic.
📋
Execution Log — a record of when those platform workflows ran and what they did, for verification and troubleshooting.
💡

Platform vs. org workflows. Rules built here apply at the platform scope. Each organisation's own admin can build org-scoped workflows separately inside their Org Admin area — the two don't collide.

11 Platform settings

Platform Settings holds configuration that cascades to every organisation. The guiding rule: org admins can add their own settings on top of these defaults, but they can't remove what you set at the platform level.

Platform Admin · Platform Settings
📧
Email Filter Defaults — baseline tags/filters that apply to every org's email handling. Org admins see these as read-only defaults in their own Email Settings and can layer their own on top.
🔌
CRM Integrations — global feature gates for the CRM connectors. This is where the platform's Salesforce write-back capability is switched on or off.
🧠
AI Providers — platform-level AI provider configuration that underpins AI features across orgs.
⚠️

A platform gate is permission, not activation. Enabling Salesforce write-back here does not turn it on for any organisation — it only allows org admins to enable it. Each org must still switch write-back on themselves in their Salesforce settings.
