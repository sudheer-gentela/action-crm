End-User Guide

Your day-to-day playbook for GoWarmCRM — working the action queue, running prospecting and sequences, logging calls and meetings, and keeping your deals moving.

No sections match your search. Try a different term.

01 Your workspace

GoWarmCRM organises your sidebar into three groups so the things you use most are always one click away. Everything else lives behind the module launcher.

app.gowarmcrm.com
Your sidebar — grouped for the work you do most.
  1. The Pipeline, Workflow, and Resources groups hold your core screens. Actions is the default landing spot — it's where your day starts.
  2. More (⋮⋮) opens the module launcher. Modules your org has enabled — Prospecting, CLM, Handovers, Service, Agency — live here. Pin the ones you use daily and they'll appear as their own sidebar item.
  3. Your user card holds your profile, notifications, and sign-out. If you also hold an admin role, you'll switch roles from here too.
💡

Pin Prospecting. If outreach is part of your day, open More and pin Prospecting so it sits in your sidebar permanently — you'll be in it constantly.

02 The action queue

The action queue is the heart of GoWarmCRM. Every night (and in real time as things change), the platform analyses your deals, prospects, and accounts and surfaces the highest-leverage things for you to do today — already prioritised, with the context attached.

Actions
Sample actions shown. Yours reflect your live pipeline.
  1. Each card shows its priority (high / medium / low) and the source it came from — a deal, prospect, account, or a strategic plan (STRAP).
  2. The title and signal tell you what happened and why it matters — for example, a deal that's crossed your team's "gone quiet" threshold.
  3. Move an action along with the status controls: Start marks it in progress, Complete ✓ closes it, and Snooze hides it until a time you choose. Finished items show with a strikethrough.
  4. Switch between the card view (great for focused work) and the table view (great for sorting and bulk scanning, with sortable, filterable columns).

Where actions come from

SourceExamples
💼 DealStalled deals, close-date risks, missing stakeholders, slow responses.
🔭 ProspectHot-lead replies to answer fast, ghosting follow-ups, wrong-channel nudges.
🏢 AccountExpansion openings, renewals approaching, whitespace to cover.
🎯 STRAPSteps from your strategic account plans — goals, strategies, and action plans.
ℹ️

The thresholds are set by your admin. If the queue feels too noisy or too quiet, that's a tuning conversation with your Org Admin — see Diagnostic Rules in the Org Admin guide.

Adding your own action

You're not limited to what the system surfaces. Create a manual action with a title, type, priority, due date, and a link to the relevant deal, account, or contact — handy for commitments you make on a call that you want tracked alongside everything else.

03 Working an action

Open any action to see the full picture — the signal that raised it, an AI insight on what to do, the email or meeting that triggered it, and suggested content you can use or adapt.

Action detail
  1. The Signal is the factual trigger — the data that crossed a threshold.
  2. The AI Insight interprets it and suggests an approach (when AI enhancement is enabled for your org).
  3. A suggested message gives you a starting draft to send as-is or edit.
  4. Quick links jump you to the email thread, deal, meeting, files, or contract; Log activity records what you did off-platform — a call, WhatsApp, LinkedIn touch, Slack message, document sent, or an internal task — and can complete the action in one step.
💡

Logging the right channel keeps your activity history accurate, which feeds the next round of diagnostics — so the queue gets smarter about you over time.

05 Contacts & Accounts

Contacts are the people; Accounts are the companies. Both give you searchable lists and detail views, and both connect back to the deals and actions that involve them.

Use Contacts to find a person, see their history, and reach them. Use Accounts to view a company's whole footprint — its contacts, deals, and the team coverage across it. If GoWarmCRM spots likely duplicates, it surfaces them so you can merge rather than work two half-records.

💡

Keeping contact roles accurate on a deal pays off — multi-threading and stakeholder coverage are part of how deal health is scored.

07 Campaigns & research

A campaign is a batch of prospects you run a coordinated outreach motion against. New prospects usually land in research first, where you (or the system) enrich and qualify them before they go live.

The flow

  1. Add prospects to a campaign — manually, by import, or captured from LinkedIn via the Chrome extension.
  2. They enter the Research Queue, where firmographic enrichment and ICP scoring fill in the picture and tell you who's worth pursuing.
  3. Activate qualified prospects into the campaign — they're enrolled into its sequence and outreach begins on schedule.
ℹ️

You can activate prospects in bulk, and a campaign's sending is paced and budgeted so you don't blast everyone at once or burn your sender reputation.

💡

Trust the ICP score. It's your org's definition of a good-fit prospect, scored automatically. Prioritise the high scorers — low-fit outreach is exactly what the prospecting diagnostics will flag later.

08 Sequences

Sequences are the multi-step cadences your outreach follows — the ordered series of emails, calls, and other touches, each with its own timing. When a prospect is enrolled, GoWarmCRM works the steps for them on schedule.

Prospecting · Sequences
A sequence is an ordered, timed series of touches.

Each step has a channel and a delay, so the cadence paces itself. The message content is personalised per prospect — drawing on their profile, your outreach style, and any custom fields — so a sequence stays personal at scale rather than feeling like a blast. You can preview drafts before they go and edit anything that doesn't fit.

ℹ️

Replies pull a prospect out of the automated cadence and into your Inbox so a human takes over at the moment it matters.

09 The prospecting inbox

The Inbox is where conversations land. It brings your outreach activity and replies together so you can respond without hunting through your email client.

📋
Activity — a running feed of what's happening across your prospects.
✉️
Email Inbox — the replies to your outreach, ready to answer in context.
📝
Drafts — generated messages waiting for your review before they send.
💡

Clear your Drafts daily — reviewing and releasing them keeps your sequences moving and gives you a last human check on every message.

10 Calls & the softphone

If your org has set up calling, you can dial prospects right from GoWarmCRM using a browser softphone — no separate phone app — and every call is logged against the prospect automatically.

Call
  1. Start a call from a prospect or a call action. The softphone connects in your browser — mute and hang up are right there, with a live timer.
  2. When you wrap up, log the outcome (connected, voicemail, no answer…) and add notes. Saving records the call and can complete the related action in one go.
ℹ️

Depending on your org's setup, calls connect via the browser softphone, a bridge to your phone, or both. Set your personal phone number in Settings if your org uses bridge calling. The Calls view in Prospecting collects your call list and history in one place.

11 The Chrome extension

The GoWarmCRM Chrome extension lets you capture prospects from LinkedIn without leaving the page — turning a profile you're looking at into a prospect in your pipeline in a couple of clicks.

  1. Install the extension and sign in with your GoWarmCRM account.
  2. On a LinkedIn profile, capture the person — their details flow into GoWarmCRM, and you can edit fields inline before saving.
  3. Pick the campaign and sequence to add them to, and they're enrolled — ready to work like any other prospect.
ℹ️

Captured profiles can be refreshed to pull updates, and you'll see an activity timeline and connection status on the prospect inside GoWarmCRM. Anything you capture shows up in your research queue for qualification.

12 Email

Connect your mailbox so GoWarmCRM can match the right messages to your deals, prospects, and accounts — and let you read and reply in context.

Once your email account is connected, relevant threads appear on the records they relate to (and in your prospecting Inbox). Your org's email filters keep newsletters and internal noise out, so what you see is the correspondence that actually matters to a deal.

💡

Email matching is what powers the "triggering email" you see on many actions — connecting your mailbox makes the whole queue richer.

13 Calendar

Your connected calendar keeps meetings visible inside GoWarmCRM and tied to the deals and contacts they involve.

See your upcoming events, and schedule meetings straight from an action or a deal (the "Schedule meeting" quick link). Meetings you hold become the raw material for transcript analysis once they're recorded.

14 Meetings & transcripts

Turn your calls and meetings into structured intelligence. GoWarmCRM can take a meeting transcript and pull out a summary, the key points, and the follow-up actions — then attach them to the right deal.

  1. Get a transcript in — either upload one, or connect a transcript provider (Zoom, Teams, Fireflies) so recordings flow in automatically.
  2. GoWarmCRM analyses it — producing a summary and surfacing what was decided and what needs doing.
  3. The resulting follow-ups can become actions in your queue, so nothing said in the room gets lost.
💡

Transcript analysis also feeds deal health and the action queue — a meeting where the economic buyer showed up, or where the close date slipped, changes the picture the system has of your deal.

15 Files

Files keeps deal documents in one place. If your org connected cloud storage (Google Drive or OneDrive), you can attach and open documents without leaving the deal.

Documents live on the deals they belong to, so the proposal, the security questionnaire, and the signed order form are all where you'd expect to find them.

16 AI Agent inbox

When your org enables the AI agent, it can go a step further than suggestions and prepare proposed actions for you to approve. The Agents screen is where you review them.

  1. The agent surfaces proposals — drafted actions or messages it thinks should happen next.
  2. Approve to let it proceed, or Reject if it's off. You're always the final say.
ℹ️

Nothing the agent proposes happens without your approval. Reviewing proposals also teaches your team where the agent is helpful and where it needs tighter guardrails.

17 Playbooks

Playbooks are the proven plays your team runs on deals and prospects. As a rep you mostly experience them as the structured steps that show up on your work — but the Playbooks screen lets you see what's available.

The steps a playbook defines flow into your action queue and your deal's Plays panel, so you're always running your team's best practice without having to remember it. Your admin manages which playbooks exist.

18 Reporting

Reporting shows how you and your team are tracking — activity, pipeline, and outcomes — so you can see what's working and where to push.

If you manage people, your reporting rolls up your team based on the hierarchy your admin configured, so you can see across your reports as well as your own numbers.

19 Your settings

Your personal settings tailor GoWarmCRM to you — your connected accounts, how outreach sounds in your voice, and your preferences. These are yours alone and don't affect teammates.

What you can set

📞

🕐

Set your outreach style early. A few minutes teaching GoWarmCRM your voice makes every generated draft feel like you wrote it — and saves you editing later.

20 Tips & shortcuts

Small habits that make GoWarmCRM faster.

  • Start on Actions. It opens there for a reason — clearing your queue is the highest-leverage thing you can do each morning.
  • Snooze instead of ignoring. If something genuinely isn't actionable yet, snooze it to when it is, so it leaves your view without being forgotten.
  • Log the channel you actually used. Accurate activity makes tomorrow's queue smarter about you.
  • Use the table view to triage. Sort by priority or due date when the queue is long, then switch to cards to work.
  • Pin the modules you live in. Prospecting especially — keep it one click away.
  • Review your Drafts daily. A quick human check keeps your sequences moving and on-message.
  • Keep your bookmarks. Each screen lives at its own URL (the address updates as you navigate), so you can bookmark a view or share a link.
🧭

Need to change how the queue behaves, your stages, or integrations? Those are admin settings — ask whoever holds the Org Admin role on your team.
